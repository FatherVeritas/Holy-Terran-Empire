Teeming With Life

Though the depths of the cosmos may be vast, they are by no means barren and void. Rather, space is a resplendent garden of resources, thoughtfully created by God for the flourishing of mankind. Far from being a cold wasteland, the deep reaches are brimming with ores, rare elements, energy sources, and raw materials waiting to be harnessed. The same Creator who lavished the Earth with abundance has scattered inexhaustible riches among the stars, calling man to rise up, explore, and cultivate this cosmic inheritance. As man civilizes the stars, he fulfills the ancient mandate to “be fruitful and multiply, fill the Earth and subdue it” — extended now to the heavens themselves.

Stellar Resources

Across the star systems, man has learned to tap the bounty of even the most extreme worlds. The vast atmospheres of gas giants are harvested for Deuterium — a stable isotope of hydrogen — providing a nearly limitless fuel source for fusion reactors and deep-space engines. Meanwhile, the sprawling asteroid belts that encircle countless suns are nature’s richest mines, teeming with every mineral imaginable: gold, platinum, lithium, and rare earth elements that once drove the engines of industry on Earth. Most prized of all are the elusive Aetherite crystals — resonant gems found in the oldest asteroids, essential for navigating the currents of Etherspace. In this grand design, nothing in the galaxy is wasted; every swirling gas cloud and drifting rock becomes part of mankind’s unfolding mission to unlock the treasures of creation and build civilizations among the stars.

Habitable Planets

Among this celestial garden lie thousands of habitable planets, each one unique in its promise. Some worlds are home to indigenous populations of rational beings, others are like empty gardens, waiting to be inhabited, tilled, and made fruitful by human hands. And beyond these known worlds stretch untold thousands more, orbiting distant suns and waiting to reveal their secrets. How many other civilizations, cultures, and yet-unseen alien races dwell beyond our reach, waiting to be discovered, befriended, and brought into the fold of Holy Mother Church, only God knows.

Perfect Conditions

Remarkably, every habitable planet ever charted shares astonishing similarities with Earth, differing by only the smallest percentages in conditions like atmosphere, gravity, and temperature. It is as if each world has been marvelously engineered to sustain the same biochemical life found on our home planet. Planets farther from their suns might possess thicker atmospheres or geothermal activity to maintain warmth, while those nearer are shielded by cloud cover or reflective surfaces to balance solar intensity. Whether nestled in desert systems or orbiting twin stars, these planets offer the perfect blend of conditions for life to thrive — an enduring testament to the wisdom and generosity of their Creator.
